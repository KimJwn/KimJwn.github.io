# Mexico Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- `Federal Criminal Code AI intimate-content bill` proposes adding Article 199 Octies to address creation of intimate content through artificial intelligence. It is a legislative proposal in the current corpus, not yet treated here as in-force criminal law. (Ref1)
- `Labor/Copyright performers AI-rights reform` was published in the Diario Oficial de la Federacion on 14 May 2026 and requires performer/artist labor contracts to specify conditions and remuneration for use of image or voice through AI systems or other technology. (Ref2, Ref3)

## Summary
- Mexico's current local corpus shows two relevant tracks: a proposed criminal-law response to AI-generated intimate content and an enacted labor/copyright protection track for performers' image and voice in AI uses. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- The enacted track is not a general deepfake statute, but it is directly relevant to consent, likeness, voice, and remuneration in synthetic-media production contexts. (Ref2, Ref3)

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: no dedicated law establishes a general deepfake-labeling mandate in the current Mexico corpus. The performer-rights reform is more about contractual consent/conditions/remuneration than public labeling of generated media. (Ref2, Ref3)
- `Election-related status`: no dedicated law for election deepfakes is identified in the current Mexico files. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: the criminal-code bill directly targets AI-created intimate content, but it remains proposal-stage in this corpus. The enacted labor/copyright reform strengthens consent-based control over image/voice use for performers. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: no dedicated law establishes a platform-duty regime for deepfake services in the current files. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: draft/proposal-stage and weak: criminal sanctions for AI intimate content are proposed in the Federal Criminal Code amendment and should remain weak/proposed until official enactment evidence is added. (Ref1)

## Practice Notes (by Dimension)
- `Labeling`: treat disclosure as an internal policy control; current Mexico files do not yet supply a general statutory labeling rule.
- `Election`: keep election synthetic-media cases under high-risk review pending dedicated official sources.
- `Non-consensual deepfakes`: separate proposed criminal exposure from enacted performer image/voice consent rights.
- `Service-provider/platform liability`: maintain notice/action logs, but do not overstate a dedicated Mexico platform-duty regime from the current corpus.
- `Penalty`: mark criminal penalties as proposed unless later DOF or consolidated code text confirms enactment.
