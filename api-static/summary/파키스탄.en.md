# Pakistan Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- The PECA framework and its 2025 amendment form the core legal structure for cybercrime/platform-governance enforcement in Pakistan. (Ref1, Ref2)
- In current corpus, deepfake governance appears integrated into existing cybercrime and platform-regulation powers rather than built as one fully stand-alone deepfake code. (Ref1, Ref2)
- The national AI policy draft provides directional context, but it must be separated from final enforceable legal obligations. (Ref3)

## Summary
- The strongest current feature is enforceability through combined criminal and platform-regulatory channels. Deepfake harms are therefore best managed as cross-channel enforcement risk, not a single-clause issue. (Ref1, Ref2)
- Operational readiness should prioritize full lifecycle controls: notice intake, restriction/removal, evidence retention, and authority cooperation. (Ref1)

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: current official corpus does not clearly establish one nationwide comprehensive stand-alone deepfake-labeling mandate. Labeling expectations should be managed conservatively through existing cyber/platform channels, while keeping internal disclosure standards ready for rapid remapping when final text evolves. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- `Election-related status`: explicit evidence of a stand-alone election-deepfake statute remains limited in current resources. Election-risk governance should therefore be handled through broader cybercrime/public-information control pathways until dedicated official election-law anchors are added. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: non-consensual and harmful synthetic-content behavior appears addressable through combined criminal and platform-enforcement pathways in current corpus. Given harm-speed dynamics, immediate operational controls should accompany legal interpretation. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: platform obligations and authority-led removal/blocking pathways are comparatively clear in the amendment-centered structure. Compliance quality depends on whether intake-decision-action-record-cooperation chains are operational and auditable. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: sanctions are codified through cybercrime/platform governance channels, but final precision on authority scope, penalty depth, and commencement should be refreshed against latest promulgated text on a scheduled basis. (Ref1, Ref2)

## Practice Notes (by Dimension)
- `Labeling`: maintain internal disclosure standards now and keep policy configuration update-ready for finalized legal text.
- `Election`: run election-context synthetic media under a distinct high-risk governance stream.
- `Non-consensual deepfakes`: enforce one-pass SOPs for intake, immediate action, evidence retention, and recirculation suppression.
- `Service-provider/platform liability`: keep one audit chain linking notice intake, action, appeal/grievance, records, and authority cooperation.
- `Penalty`: operate dual exposure matrices (criminal + administrative/regulatory) per incident category.
