# Saudi Arabia Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- `Anti-Cyber Crime Law (Royal Decree No. M/17, 2007)` is the primary legal anchor covering privacy invasion, reputation harms, and unlawful dissemination in digital settings. (Ref1)
- The local corpus also includes a deepfake guide, indicating a law-plus-guidance structure where operational interpretation supplements statutory enforcement. (Ref2)

## Summary
- In current Saudi corpus, deepfake governance is primarily enforced by fitting conduct into existing cybercrime categories rather than relying on one dedicated deepfake offense chapter. (Ref1)
- Operationally, this means incident handling should classify both synthetic-media characteristics and underlying offense categories (privacy/reputation/unlawful dissemination) in parallel. (Ref1, Ref2)

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: current official resources do not clearly establish one comprehensive statutory deepfake-labeling mandate at article level. Guidance-level direction exists, so services should run proactive internal disclosure standards and keep update pathways ready for future hard-law clarification. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Election-related status`: explicit stand-alone election-deepfake codification is limited in current corpus. Election-risk handling should therefore remain conservative and be managed through broader cybercrime/public-order enforcement pathways. (Ref1)
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: non-consensual synthetic harms can be addressed through combined privacy, reputation, and unlawful dissemination provisions under cybercrime law. Given harm-speed dynamics, legal interpretation should be paired with immediate operational controls. (Ref1)
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: platform responsibility appears governed through general cybercrime enforcement architecture rather than a highly granular stand-alone platform-duty code. Services should demonstrate compliance through robust intake-decision-action-record chains, using guidance as operational support. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: sanctions are primarily applied through existing cybercrime penalties instead of deepfake-specific offense text. Practical penalty exposure therefore depends on accurate offense-category mapping during incident classification. (Ref1)

Ref1: `sources/official/saudi-arabia/saudi-2007-Anti-Cyber-Crime-Law-M17-EN.pdf`
Ref2: `sources/official/saudi-arabia/saudi-AI-DeepFake-Guide-EN-2021.pdf`

## Practice Notes (by Dimension)
- `Labeling`: run internal disclosure controls now (wording/placement/persistence) with legal-update hooks.
- `Election`: process election-context synthetic media under a dedicated high-risk review stream.
- `Non-consensual deepfakes`: enforce one-pass SOPs for intake, immediate action, evidence retention, and recirculation control.
- `Service-provider/platform liability`: maintain a single audit chain linking notice intake, action, records, and cooperation outputs.
- `Penalty`: use offense-mapping matrices that jointly evaluate privacy, reputation, and unlawful dissemination risk categories.
