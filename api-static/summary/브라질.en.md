# Brazil Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- Brazil is represented in the current corpus through draft AI-governance materials and official legislative pages, including PL 2338/2023 and PL 2688/2025. These materials are treated as draft/proposed evidence unless a final enacted text is added. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- The current Brazil corpus is therefore useful for tracking an emerging regulatory pathway, but it should not be presented as a consolidated in-force deepfake statute. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)

## Summary
- Brazil's current materials route synthetic-media harms through proposed AI governance, transparency, rights protection, and platform/accountability concepts rather than a single enacted deepfake-specific law. (Ref1, Ref2, Ref3)
- For comparison, Brazil should be coded as an emerging Americas pathway with draft legal force in the current working corpus.

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: draft/proposal-stage and weak: the current Brazil materials support transparency and AI-governance analysis, but the corpus does not yet confirm an in-force general deepfake-labeling rule.
- `Election-related status`: unclear/no dedicated rule in the current Brazil corpus: additional official election-law sources should be checked before coding an election-specific pathway.
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: unclear in the current corpus: the draft AI materials may be relevant to rights and harm prevention, but current files do not establish a dedicated NCII deepfake offense.
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: draft/proposal-stage and weak: the materials are relevant to accountability and governance, but should remain proposed until enacted obligations are confirmed.
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: draft/proposal-stage and weak: penalties or sanctions should not be coded as binding from these files alone.
