# Singapore Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- Current official local corpus includes `Elections (Integrity of Online Advertising) (Amendment) Bill 29/2024`. (Ref1)
- In this corpus, Singapore deepfake governance appears to be developing primarily through election-integrity legislation rather than one broad stand-alone deepfake law. (Ref1)

## Summary
- The strongest currently evidenced dimension is election-context synthetic/deepfake governance. Cross-domain claims should be interpreted conservatively because current official corpus is election-focused and proposal-stage. (Ref1)
- Operationally, services should prioritize election-period controls for labeling, provenance, and response workflows. (Ref1)

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: labeling obligations are visible in election online-advertising integrity context, but evidence remains limited for a broad all-domain statutory deepfake-labeling mandate. General service contexts should therefore be managed conservatively until additional official legal anchors are added. (Ref1)
- `Election-related status`: election-related synthetic/deepfake controls are explicitly under legislative development through the amendment-bill pathway and represent the clearest codification track in current corpus. (Ref1)
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: current official corpus is insufficient to confirm a stand-alone dedicated non-consensual-deepfake offense framework. This dimension should remain conservative pending additional official criminal-law sources. (Ref1)
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: platform/intermediary duties are visible in election online-advertising integrity context, while broad deepfake platform-duty codification still needs further article-level confirmation. (Ref1)
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: sanctions are tied to election-integrity legislative pathway in current corpus; final sanction mapping should be refreshed after enacted-text confirmation. (Ref1)

Ref1: `sources/official/singapore/elections-(integrity-of-online-advertising)-(amendment)-bill-29-2024.pdf`

## Practice Notes (by Dimension)
- `Labeling`: enforce fixed disclosure standards (wording/placement/persistence) for election-context synthetic content.
- `Election`: run election-period synthetic-media governance as a separate high-risk workflow.
- `Non-consensual deepfakes`: apply conservative internal response controls (rapid action + evidence retention) while legal scope is being clarified.
- `Service-provider/platform liability`: document notice-action-record-escalation chains aligned to election-integrity requirements.
- `Penalty`: track bill progression and update sanctions matrices immediately when final enacted text is published.
