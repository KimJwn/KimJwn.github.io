# Japan Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- Japan is represented in the current corpus by two official legal texts: the 2025 AI Technology Act and the Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI), amended at April 2026. (Ref1, Ref2)
- These are adjacent legal frameworks rather than a dedicated deepfake statute. They support analysis of AI governance, data protection, privacy, and accountability pathways.

## Summary
- Japan's current materials show a governance-oriented and adjacent-law pathway: synthetic-media harms are read through AI policy promotion, institutional responsibilities, personal-information protection, and privacy/data-use obligations.
- The current corpus should not be coded as confirming a comprehensive deepfake-specific law.

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: unclear/no dedicated law in the current corpus: the AI Technology Act is relevant to AI governance, but the files do not establish a general deepfake disclosure or watermarking mandate.
- `Election-related status`: no dedicated law identified in the current Japan corpus: election-specific synthetic-media rules require additional official sources.
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: adjacent-law pathway: APPI is relevant to personal information and privacy, but the current corpus does not establish a dedicated NCII deepfake offense.
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: adjacent/soft governance pathway: the current files support institutional AI-governance analysis, not a deepfake-specific platform-duty regime.
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: distributed/unclear: penalties should be linked to the specific adjacent law being applied, not coded as a standalone deepfake penalty regime.
