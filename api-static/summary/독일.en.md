# Germany Deepfake Law Summary

## Core Legal Framework
- Current Bundesrat-source corpus includes a criminal-law track addressing deepfake-linked personality-rights harms (201b StGB-related legislative path). (Ref2)
- In the present local official set, criminal-law treatment is stronger and clearer than broad nationwide labeling/platform-duty codification. (Ref1, Ref2)

## Summary
- The clearest currently evidenced German dimension is criminal-law treatment of non-consensual/personality-rights-invasive deepfake harms. Broad labeling and platform-duty claims should remain conservative until additional official sources are added. (Ref1, Ref2)
- Operationally, this implies prioritizing criminal-risk response controls now while keeping labeling/platform governance adaptable pending further statutory confirmation. (Ref1, Ref2)

## Policy Dimensions
- `Labeling status`: current official local resources are not sufficient to confirm one comprehensive nationwide deepfake-labeling mandate. Labeling claims remain context-level rather than article-level in this corpus, so services should run internal labeling standards as provisional controls and defer hard-law certainty claims until expanded official sources are available. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Election-related status`: explicit evidence of a stand-alone election-deepfake statute remains limited in the current corpus. Election-risk governance should be handled conservatively through broader criminal/misinformation/public-order control channels until dedicated official election-law sources are added. (Ref1, Ref2)
- `Non-consensual deepfake status`: this is the strongest identifiable codification track in the current German set. Criminal-law treatment of non-consensual deepfake/personality-rights harm is materially visible, supporting stronger statements in this dimension compared with others. (Ref2)
- `Service-provider/platform liability status`: article-level platform-duty codification cannot be finalized from current corpus alone. Platform controls should therefore emphasize robust internal governance and auditable response logic while official legal-source coverage is expanded. (Ref1)
- `Penalty/sanctions codification status`: a criminal sanctions track is identifiable, but final article wording, commencement timing, and enacted-text certainty still require latest federal publication cross-checks. Best current framing is: codification direction is clear; final enacted precision is pending confirmation. (Ref2, Ref1)

## Practice Notes (by Dimension)
- `Labeling`: run internal disclosure standards now (wording/placement/persistence) with future legal mapping hooks.
- `Election`: treat election-related synthetic media as a separate high-risk governance stream and predefine legal-escalation triggers.
- `Non-consensual deepfakes`: prioritize immediate block/evidence-preservation/recirculation prevention in criminal-risk SOPs.
- `Service-provider/platform liability`: compensate for source gaps by strengthening policy-to-action traceability and audit logs.
- `Penalty`: evaluate criminal exposure at intake stage and maintain a scheduled legal-update cycle for enacted-text changes.
